About Earth


Earth is the planet we live on, one of eight planets in our solar system and the only known place in the universe to support life. It is the third planet from the sun, after Mercury and Venus, and before Mars. Earth is the largest and most massive of the rocky inner planets, although it is dwarfed by the gas giants beyond the Asteroid Belt.



Structure


The structure of the earth is divided into major components: the crust, the mantle, the outer core, the inner core and its atmosphere. Each layer has a unique chemical composition, physical state, and can impact life on Earth's surface. Movement in the mantle caused by variations in heat from the core, cause the plates to shift, which can cause earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. These natural hazards then change our landscape, and in some cases, threaten lives and property. Learn more about how the earth is constructed with these classroom resources.

A. Crust

Describes as the outermost shell of a terrestrial planet. Earth's crust is generally divided into older, thicker continental crust and younger, denser oceanic crust. The dynamic geology of Earth's crust is informed by plate tectonics.

B. Mantle

The mantle is the mostly solid bulk of Earth's interior. The mantle lies between Earth's dense, super-heated core and its thin outer layer, the crust. The mantle is about 2,900 kilometers (1,802 miles) thick, and makes up a whopping 84 percent of Earth’s total volume.

C. Outer Core

The outer core, about 2,200 kilometers (1,367 miles) thick, is mostly composed of liquid iron and nickel. The NiFe alloy of the outer core is very hot, between 4,500° and 5,500° Celsius (8,132° and 9,932° Fahrenheit).

D. Inner Core

The inner core is a hot, dense ball of (mostly) iron. It has a radius of about 1,220 kilometers (758 miles). Temperature in the inner core is about 5,200° Celsius (9,392° Fahrenheit). The pressure is nearly 3.6 million atmosphere (atm).

E. Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen (about 78%) and oxygen (about 21%). It also contains trace amounts of other gases, including carbon dioxide, argon, and water vapor.

F. Hydrosphere

Earth's hydrosphere includes all forms of water on the planet, such as oceans, seas, lakes, rivers, and groundwater.
