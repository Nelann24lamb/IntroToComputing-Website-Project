About Mercury


Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun in our solar system. It is characterized by a rocky surface, heavily cratered terrain, and extreme temperature variations. With no significant atmosphere to retain heat, Mercury experiences scorching daytime temperatures and frigid nights.



Structure


A. Crust

Mercury's crust is rocky and heavily cratered, similar to the Moon's surface. It is composed of various silicate rocks.

B. Mantle

Beneath the crust lies the mantle, which is believed to be partially molten. The mantle is composed of silicate minerals, similar to Earth's mantle.

C. Core

Mercury has a relatively large and dense metallic core, primarily composed of iron and nickel. This core makes up a significant portion of the planet's mass.

D. Atmosphere

Mercury has a very thin exosphere, which is an extremely tenuous and wispy atmosphere. It contains trace amounts of hydrogen, helium, and oxygen, but it lacks a substantial atmosphere compared to Earth or Venus.
