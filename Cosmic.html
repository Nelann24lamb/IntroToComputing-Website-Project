About Solar System


The solar system is an assembly made up of the Sun, which is an average star in the Milky Way Galaxy, and the bodies that orbit it, including the comets and other icy bodies, the eight (formerly nine) planets and the more than 210 known planetary satellites (moons) that orbit each of them, numerous asteroids, some of which have their own satellites, and vast stretches of extremely tenuous gas and dust known as the interplanetary medium.

The solar system is a component of the "observable universe," which is the area of space that, because to technological advancements, humanity are able to view in real life or in theory. The universe may be limitless, in contrast to the observable universe.

The name of the Solar System is derived from the Sun's Latin name Sol, and is because of that sometimes also called Sol System.
